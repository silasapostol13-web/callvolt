Cold Call Script — Works for any industry

Goal is not to sell on the call. Goal is to book a 10-minute demo. Read this out loud 5 times before your first call.

→

The Opener — First 15 seconds decide everything

Don't improvise this part
Call ▾
Opening — say this confidently, no pausing

"Hey [First Name], this is Silas — I know I'm catching you out of the blue so I'll be quick. I help service businesses set up an AI that answers every call 24/7 and books jobs automatically. I've got a quick question — what happens when a customer calls your company after hours right now?"

Then shut up. Let them answer. Their answer tells you everything — if they say "voicemail" or "I don't know" you have your in. If they say "we have coverage" ask "and what about overflow calls when the line's busy?"
→

The Pivot to Demo

After they answer your question
Call ▾
After their answer

"That's exactly the gap I help close. I'm not going to pitch you right now — I'd rather just show you. Do you have 10 minutes sometime this week where I can call you and literally let you listen to the AI handle a call in real time? You'll know in 2 minutes if it's worth your time."

Key phrase: "Let you listen to the AI in real time." This is specific and low-commitment. They're not agreeing to a pitch — they're agreeing to listen to a phone call.
→

If They Say "Send Me Info First"

Common deflection
Call ▾
Response

"Absolutely — I'll send you a 2-minute video right now that shows the AI handling a real call. But I'll be honest with you — most people who just get info never have time to sit down and watch it. Can I grab 10 minutes with you first and then send the full breakdown after? That way you get the context to make it actually useful."

→

If They Say "Not Interested"

Don't give up immediately
Call ▾
Response — one gentle push

"Totally fair. Before I let you go — is it that AI phone agents aren't something you'd consider, or is this just bad timing? I don't want to follow up if it's genuinely not a fit, but if it's just timing I'm happy to circle back in a month or two."

Then honor whatever they say. If they say not a fit — thank them and hang up. If they say bad timing — ask what month works and put it in your calendar. Never argue.
