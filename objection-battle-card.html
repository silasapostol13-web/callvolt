Objection Battle Card

Keep this open on your phone during every sales call. Search the objection, use the response. Know the red ones cold before you make your first call.

Must know cold
Review before calls
Less common

"That's too expensive" / "I can't afford that"

Most Common▾
Acknowledge
"Fair reaction — before I respond, quick question: how many calls do you think you miss per month? Even a rough guess."
Reframe
"So if you're missing even 15 calls a month, and half would've been jobs at your average ticket... that's real revenue walking to competitors every month. The AI costs a fraction of that. You're not spending — you're recovering money you're already losing."
Close
"Let me pull up the ROI calculator right now and run your actual numbers. If the math doesn't work, I'll tell you that honestly. Sound fair?"
Always reframe cost → ROI. Never defend the price. Make them do the math on missed calls. Pull up the ROI calculator on your screen while you say this.

"My customers will hate talking to a robot"

Most Common▾
Acknowledge
"That's the right concern to have — and honestly, if it sounded like a robot, I wouldn't sell it."
Reframe
"Here's what I'd love to do — let me call a demo number right now and let you listen for 60 seconds. You decide. And keep in mind: your customers aren't comparing this to a perfect human receptionist. They're comparing it to voicemail. Voicemail loses 100% of the time."
Close
"60 second test — if it sounds robotic to you, I'll stop the pitch right here. Deal?"
The live demo kills this objection. Always be ready to call your demo line mid-conversation. Have it saved in your phone as "CallVolt Demo."

"We already have a receptionist / answering service"

Very Common▾
Acknowledge
"Great — having someone handle calls is important. Quick question: what happens when they're on another call, on lunch, or after 5 PM?"
Reframe
"This isn't about replacing them — it's about covering the gaps. The AI handles overflow, after-hours, weekends, holidays. It's the safety net that makes sure zero calls ever hit voicemail. Think of it as their backup, not their replacement."
Close
"What if we set it up just for after-hours and overflow to start? You keep your current setup during the day. Minimal change, maximum coverage."
For answering services: "How much are you paying per call? Our AI is typically 60–70% cheaper per interaction and never puts callers on hold."

"I'm not interested" / "Not a good time"

Very Common▾
One gentle push
"Totally respect that. Before I let you go — is it that AI phone agents aren't something you'd consider, or is this just bad timing? I don't want to follow up if it's genuinely not a fit, but if it's just timing I'm happy to circle back."
Then honor whatever they say. Bad timing → ask what month. Not a fit → thank them and move on. Never argue. One push max.

"I need to think about it"

Common▾
Uncover the real objection
"Of course — this is a real decision. Just so I can send you the right info: is it more the cost, whether the AI will actually work for your business, or something else?"
Set a hard next step
"Let's do this — how about a 10-minute follow-up call on [specific day]? That way you've had time to think and I can answer anything that comes up. Does [day] at [time] work?"
"Think about it" = you haven't addressed the real objection. The question about cost/tech/results usually surfaces what's actually holding them back.
Never say: "What's there to think about?" or any pressure tactic. Always leave the door open warmly and with a specific next step.

"We're too small for this" / "It's just me"

Common▾
Reframe
"Actually, you're exactly who this is built for. Big companies have call centers. You're the one answering your phone with your hands dirty while you're on a job. This is what lets you focus on the work and stop missing calls at the same time."
Close
"The ROI math actually works better for solo operators — every recovered call is pure profit with no overhead. Want me to show you what that looks like with your numbers?"

"I don't trust AI" / "I'm not a tech person"

Common▾
Acknowledge
"Respect that — there's a lot of AI hype right now and it's hard to know what's real. You don't need to trust AI. You just need to hear it work for 60 seconds and decide if it sounds good enough."
Reframe
"You don't need to understand how it works any more than you need to understand how your engine works to drive. We set it up, we manage it, you check your calendar and see booked jobs. That's it."
Focus on outcomes, never technology. "More jobs on your calendar" resonates. "AI-powered voice synthesis" does not.

"I don't want to be locked into a contract"

Moderate▾
Response
"No contract — month to month. Cancel anytime with 30 days notice. The only thing you pay upfront is a one-time setup fee, because I actually build the system custom for your business. But the monthly? You own that decision every single month. We keep you because it works."
Usually the easiest objection. No contract + month-to-month = no risk. Pair with a first-month guarantee if they're still hesitant.

"I'll just hire a receptionist instead"

Moderate▾
Acknowledge
"That's definitely an option. Have you looked at what that actually costs right now?"
Reframe
"A part-time receptionist runs $15–18/hour — that's $2,400–$2,900/month for 40 hours a week. No nights, no weekends, no holidays. They call in sick. Take lunch. Quit. And you have to manage them. The AI costs a fraction of that, works 24/7/365, and requires zero management."
Close
"Why not start with AI now — live in a week — and hire when your volume demands it? The AI can handle overflow when you do hire someone."
Do the math out loud. $15/hr × 40hrs × 4wks = $2,400/mo minimum. No after-hours. No weekends. Payroll taxes on top of that. The comparison is stark.

"Maybe after busy season" / "Not the right time"

Moderate▾
Reframe
"Busy season is actually when you need this most — that's when you're missing the most calls because you're slammed on jobs. Setting it up before the rush means you capture every call during your highest-revenue months. Setting it up after means you already missed them."
Close
"Setup takes less than a week and requires 30 minutes of your time max. If we start now, you're live before [upcoming peak]. Want to lock that in?"
Use their specific season: HVAC → summer AC calls. Pest → spring termite season. Plumbing → winter freeze. Roofing → storm season. Make the timing concrete.

"We tried something like this before and it didn't work"

Occasional▾
Ask first
"Tell me about that — what did you try? Because there's a massive difference between old IVR phone-tree systems and what we build today."
Reframe
"The voice quality, the natural conversation handling, the direct calendar booking — none of that existed 3 years ago. I'd rather you call the demo and judge it yourself than take my word for it. 60 seconds."
